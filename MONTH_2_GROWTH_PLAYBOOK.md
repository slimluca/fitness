# Month-2 Growth Playbook

Use this note once Fitness Grand Baie is live and real impressions, clicks, reviews, and leads are starting to appear.

## 1. Improve Pages With Real Data

- `Impressions up, CTR low`: improve the page title, meta description, H1, and first-screen trust message before changing the whole page.
- `Clicks up, conversions low`: tighten the first CTA block, add clearer reassurance, and make the next step more obvious above the fold.
- `WhatsApp clicks high, form fills low`: the page likely attracts warm leads who want speed. Keep WhatsApp prominent and reduce form friction rather than forcing more fields.
- `Forms high, lead quality weak`: strengthen qualification copy near the form and make the ideal client fit clearer before the form begins.
- `Blog traffic high, weak business impact`: improve article end CTAs, add more relevant service links, and move stronger money-page prompts higher inside the article.
- `Pricing traffic high, weak action`: add more reassurance around service fit, privacy, and what happens after enquiry rather than only changing prices or package layout.
- `Homepage traffic high, weak action`: sharpen the hero promise, trust message, and first CTA row first.
- `Mauritius landing page traffic high, weak action`: reinforce private coaching fit, pricing direction, and the best next step for broad-intent visitors.
- `Grand Baie landing page traffic high, weak action`: strengthen local convenience, villa/expat/tourist fit, and the fastest WhatsApp or availability route.
- `Service-page traffic high, weak action`: tighten the service intro, add stronger proof near the top, and make the inquiry route easier to trust.

## 2. Where To Insert The First Real Reviews

Replace the first proof slots in this order:

1. Homepage: 2 to 3 concise reviews that mention privacy, professionalism, and consistency.
2. `\/personal-trainer-mauritius`: 1 broader Mauritius-facing review plus 1 Grand Baie-specific private-coaching review.
3. `\/services/private-personal-training`: 2 reviews that explain why one-to-one private coaching felt better than a public gym.
4. `\/pricing`: 1 review that makes the investment feel justified through service quality and consistency.
5. `\/results`: the strongest longer-form quote with a clear before/after story or measurable outcome.
6. `\/contact`: 1 short reassurance-focused review that helps serious leads trust the next step.
7. `\/about`: 1 review that reinforces professionalism, coaching style, and the quality of the private environment.

Recommended review structure:

- Privacy-safe display name: full name, first name, initials, or private label.
- Service used: private training, couple training, house calls, or online coaching.
- Short result summary: routine, strength, confidence, fat loss, posture, or adherence.
- Optional context: Grand Baie resident, expat, villa guest, busy professional, beginner.
- Optional source label: Google review, direct testimonial, private client feedback.

## 5. Lead-Source Decision Support

Use lead origin to decide what to improve next:

- Homepage leads: often broad-intent and brand-trust driven. If weak quality, clarify who the service is for sooner.
- Pricing leads: often high-intent but comparing options. If weak quality, improve service-fit guidance and package context.
- Service-page leads: usually warmer and more specific. If low conversion, improve proof near the top and make the inquiry route clearer.
- Mauritius landing-page leads: often comparing coaches and formats. If weak, sharpen private-coaching differentiation and pricing guidance.
- Grand Baie landing-page leads: often local, expat, or short-stay intent. If weak, reinforce local convenience and quick availability routes.
- Blog-origin leads: often colder but educable. If quality is weak, tighten article-to-service pathways and more precise end CTAs.
- WhatsApp-heavy leads: often time-sensitive or higher urgency. Protect speed and clarity rather than forcing longer forms.
- Form-heavy leads: often more thoughtful and higher-context. Use those leads to refine service fit, objections, and qualification questions.
- Tourist or expat signals: usually value convenience, speed, and clarity around short stays or changing schedules.
- Local resident signals: often care more about long-term routine, premium privacy, and schedule consistency.
